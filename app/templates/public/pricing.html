How to choose

Choose Chat if...

  • You mainly want a texting companion experience.
  • You want the relationship, memory, and parent portal without prioritizing voice.
  • You want a lower monthly starting point.

How to choose

Choose Voice if...

  • Voice expression would make the relationship much more meaningful.
  • Your child would enjoy a companion that can sound playful, musical, or emotionally expressive.
  • You want the fullest version of the Resona experience.

How billing works

Each plan includes monthly credits. If your family needs more than the included amount, additional credits can be purchased for an added cost so usage stays transparent instead of mysterious.

Included monthly credits

Chat includes $10 in monthly credits. Voice includes $30. Those credits help cover the ongoing companion experience before extra billing is needed.

Additional credits

If your family uses more than the included amount, additional credits can be purchased instead of forcing a plan change too early. The goal is flexibility and clarity, not surprise bills.

What happens if you add another child?

Each household starts with one active child profile included. That child gets one active Resona. If your family wants multiple active child profiles on the same account, each additional one is billed as a $12/month add-on. There is no separate extra-persona charge beyond the child seat.

How seat changes work

Parents add, archive, and restore child profiles inside the portal. Active profiles count toward billing, while archived profiles stay on record without continuing as active paid seats.

What your monthly price is buying

The subscription itself

The monthly plan covers access to Resona for one active child profile, the parent portal, long-term memory, routines, safety visibility, and the overall companion experience. That same included child also gets one active Resona. It is not only paying for raw API usage.

What credits are for

The ongoing live usage

Credits are simply the usage budget that powers the live experience behind the scenes: texting, memory work, parent-chat generation, and voice processing. They exist so families can see what is included clearly and purchase more if they need it.
